# GRIHA V2015 — Green Rating for Integrated Habitat Assessment (2015)

> **Type:** Star Rating (1 to 5 Stars)
> **Total Points:** 100 (base) + 4 (innovation) = **104 pts**
> **Applies to:** New construction buildings in India

## Section Descriptions

### Section 1 — Site Planning (8 pts)
Site selection for buildability and minimal ecological disruption, low-impact design for stormwater management (permeable surfaces, bioswales), Urban Heat Island Effect mitigation (cool roofs, SRI-rated surfaces), and site imperviousness factor to limit hard paving coverage.

**Key criteria:** Site Selection (1 pt), Low-impact design (4 pts), Design to mitigate UHIE (2 pts), Site Imperviousness Factor (1 pt)

### Section 2 — Construction Management (9 pts)
Air and water pollution control during construction, preservation and protection of existing landscape (trees, water bodies) during the build, and comprehensive construction management practices including C&D waste management.

**Key criteria:** Air and water pollution control (1 pt), Preserve and protect landscape (4 pts), Construction Management Practices (4 pts)

### Section 3 — Energy (20 pts)
Largest base section. Building energy efficiency improvement over baseline through passive design + efficient systems (13 pts), and on-site renewable energy generation (7 pts). Zero-ODP materials is a mandatory requirement (0 pts).

**Key criteria:** Energy efficiency (13 pts), Renewable energy utilization (7 pts), Zero ODP materials (Mandatory)

### Section 4 — Occupant Comfort and Well Being (12 pts)
Achieving indoor comfort in visual (daylighting, illuminance), thermal (temperature, humidity), and acoustic (noise levels) dimensions. Maintaining good Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) through ventilation and pollutant control. Using low-VOC paints and compounds in all interior spaces.

**Key criteria:** Indoor comfort requirements — visual/thermal/acoustic (6 pts), Maintaining good IAQ (4 pts), Low-VOC paints and compounds (2 pts)

### Section 5 — Water (17 pts)
Low-flow fixtures to reduce consumption, strategies to reduce landscape/irrigation water demand (native planting), water quality maintenance, on-site water reuse (greywater, STP effluent), and rainwater recharge through percolation structures.

**Key criteria:** Low-flow fixtures (4 pts), Landscape water demand reduction (4 pts), Water Quality (2 pts), On-site water reuse (5 pts), Rainwater Recharge (2 pts)

### Section 6 — Sustainable Building Materials (14 pts)
Use of BIS-recommended industrial waste materials in the building structure (fly ash, GGBS, bottom ash), reduction in embodied energy of the structure, and use of low-environmental-impact materials in interiors (low-VOC finishes, sustainably sourced timber).

**Key criteria:** BIS-recommended waste materials in structure (6 pts), Reduction in embodied energy (4 pts), Low-environmental impact interior materials (4 pts)

### Section 7 — Solid Waste Management (6 pts)
Post-construction landfill diversion through C&D waste recycling and reuse, and on-site treatment of organic waste from building operations (composting, biogas).

**Key criteria:** Avoided post-construction landfill (4 pts), Treat organic waste on site (2 pts)

### Section 8 — Socio-Economic Strategies (6 pts)
Labour safety and sanitation for construction workers, design for universal accessibility, dedicated facilities for service staff (rest rooms, canteen), and increasing environmental awareness among building occupants.

**Key criteria:** Labour safety and sanitation (1 pt), Universal Accessibility (2 pts), Dedicated facilities for service staff (2 pts), Environmental awareness (1 pt)

### Section 9 — Performance Monitoring and Validation (8 pts)
Smart metering and monitoring system for real-time energy and water tracking (8 pts). Operation and maintenance protocols and final performance assessment are mandatory prerequisites with no points.

**Key criteria:** Smart metering and monitoring (8 pts), O&M Protocols (Mandatory), Performance Assessment for Final Rating (Mandatory)

### Section 10 — Innovation (4 pts, Bonus)
Up to 4 bonus points for strategies exceeding GRIHA V2015 criteria or addressing sustainability dimensions not in the rating system.

**Key criteria:** Innovation (4 pts)
