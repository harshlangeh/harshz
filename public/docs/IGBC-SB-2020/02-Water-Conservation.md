## WC MR 1 — Rainwater Harvesting, Roof (Mandatory)

### Intent
Reduce stormwater runoff and dependency on municipal water by capturing roof rainwater.

**Mandatory Requirement (No points — must comply):**
A basic rainwater harvesting system must be installed to capture and store/recharge roof runoff. Minimum standards per local government bye-laws apply. In many states (Karnataka, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Delhi), rainwater harvesting is already mandatory under state law.

**Minimum System Elements:**
- Gutters and downpipes connected to all roof areas
- First-flush diverter (divert first 1–2 mm of rainfall to drain — carries dust, bird droppings)
- Sand/gravel filter
- Storage tank OR recharge pit

## WC Credit 1 — Rainwater Harvesting, Roof (4 pts New / 3 pts Existing)

### Points Scale (New Buildings)

| System Performance | Points |
|-------------------|--------|
| System captures rainfall for ≥ 1 month per year of non-potable demand | 1 |
| System captures rainfall for ≥ 2 months per year of non-potable demand | 2 |
| System captures rainfall for ≥ 3 months per year of non-potable demand | 3 |
| System captures rainfall for ≥ 4 months per year of non-potable demand | 4 |

### Points Scale (Existing Buildings)

| System Performance | Points |
|-------------------|--------|
| System captures rainfall for ≥ 1 month per year | 1 |
| ≥ 2 months | 2 |
| ≥ 3 months or more | 3 |

### Rainwater Calculation Methodology
```
Monthly Harvest Potential (m³) = Monthly Rainfall (mm) × Roof Area (m²) × 0.001 × 0.85
Monthly Non-Potable Demand (m³) = Occupants × Non-Potable Use (L/day) × 30 × 0.001
```
Example: 500 m² roof, 60 mm/month rainfall, 50 occupants using 45 L/day non-potable:
- Monthly harvest = 500 × 60 × 0.001 × 0.85 = 25.5 m³
- Monthly demand = 50 × 45 × 30 × 0.001 = 67.5 m³
- System covers = 25.5/67.5 = 38% of demand for that month

**Documentation:** Monthly rainfall data (IMD), roof area calculations, storage tank sizing, demand calculations.

## WC Credit 2 — Water Efficient Plumbing Fixtures (3 pts New / 3 pts Existing)

### Points Scale (% Reduction in Indoor Water Use vs. IGBC Baseline)

| Water Savings | Points |
|--------------|--------|
| ≥ 20% reduction | 1 |
| ≥ 35% reduction | 2 |
| ≥ 50% reduction | 3 |

### Baseline vs. Efficient Fixtures

| Fixture | IGBC Baseline | Efficient Target | Savings |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|---------|
| WC | 6 L/flush | 3 L/flush (dual flush avg) | 50% |
| Urinals | 3 L/flush | 0.5 L/flush (sensor) | 83% |
| Washbasin | 8 L/min | 4 L/min (aerator) | 50% |
| Shower | 10 L/min | 6 L/min | 40% |

**Documentation:** Fixture specifications with flow rates, calculation worksheet showing % savings vs. IGBC baseline.

## WC Credit 3 — Water Metering (1 pt New / 1 pt Existing)

**Requirements for 1 point:**
- Individual water sub-meters installed for each of the following (where applicable):
  - Domestic potable water
  - HVAC makeup water (cooling tower)
  - Landscape/irrigation
  - STP feed (if on-site treatment exists)
- Meter readings logged monthly and available for review

**Documentation:** Plumbing drawing showing sub-meter locations, 12-month consumption log (for existing buildings).
