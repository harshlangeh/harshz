# Section 4 — Building Materials and Resources

**Rating System:** IGBC SB 2020
**Maximum Points:** 8 (New) / 1 (Existing)

> **Note:** This section heavily favours New Buildings. Existing buildings can only earn 1 point (Green Procurement Policy). All other credits are N/A for Existing Buildings.

## BMR MR 1 — Segregation of Waste, Post-occupancy (Mandatory)

All buildings must provide:
- Colour-coded waste bins on each floor (green = organic/wet, blue = dry/recyclable, red = hazardous/e-waste)
- Centralised waste collection area with ventilation and pest control
- Agreement with a registered recycler or waste management agency
- Compliance with Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016

No points — mandatory gate requirement.

---

## BMR Credit 1 — Green Procurement Policy (N/A for New / 1 pt for Existing)

**Only applicable to Existing Buildings.**

1 pt for developing and implementing a formal Green Procurement Policy covering:
- Preference for eco-labelled or certified products (Energy Star, BEE 5-star, ISO 14001 suppliers)
- Elimination of harmful chemicals from cleaning products (replaced by eco-certified alternatives)
- Preference for locally manufactured products (within 500 km) to reduce transport emissions
- Documented policy reviewed annually and signed by senior management

**Documentation:** Signed Green Procurement Policy document, procurement records showing compliance.

## BMR Credit 2 — Eco-labelled Building Materials, Products & Equipment (3 pts New / N/A Existing)

### Intent
To use materials and products that have been independently certified for reduced environmental impact throughout their life cycle.

### Points Scale (% of total material cost using eco-labelled products)

| % Eco-labelled Materials | Points |
|--------------------------|--------|
| ≥ 10% | 1 |
| ≥ 20% | 2 |
| ≥ 30% | 3 |

### Accepted Eco-Labels in India

| Label | Scope |
|-------|-------|
| **BIS Eco Mark** | Indian eco-label for products meeting IS standards with environmental criteria |
| **GreenPro** (CII IGBC) | Building materials certified by IGBC for green performance |
| **Energy Star** | Electrical equipment energy efficiency |
| **BEE Star Label** | BEE rating for appliances and equipment |
| **ISO 14001** | Manufacturer's environmental management system |
| **FSC / PEFC** | Timber from sustainably managed forests |
| **EPD (Environmental Product Declaration)** | Third-party verified lifecycle data for building products |

## BMR Credit 3 — Alternative Construction Technologies & Materials (3 pts New / N/A Existing)

### Intent
To reduce the use of conventional resource-intensive materials (fired clay bricks, virgin stone aggregate, OPC concrete) by substituting alternatives that use industrial by-products or require less energy to produce.

### Points Scale

| % Alternative Materials in Structure | Points |
|--------------------------------------|--------|
| ≥ 25% | 1 |
| ≥ 40% | 2 |
| ≥ 60% | 3 |

### Accepted Alternative Technologies and Materials

| Technology / Material | Environmental Benefit |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Precast concrete** | Reduces formwork waste; factory quality control |
| **Fly ash bricks/blocks** | Eliminates topsoil loss from brick kilns; uses coal power waste |
| **AAC (Autoclaved Aerated Concrete) blocks** | 60% lighter than conventional concrete; good insulation |
| **GGBS concrete** | Replaces 50–70% of OPC; reduces embodied carbon by ~40% |
| **PPC (Portland Pozzolana Cement)** | Contains 25–35% fly ash; now standard in India |
| **Bamboo (engineered)** | Renewable; tensile strength approaching steel |
| **Compressed Stabilised Earth Blocks (CSEB)** | Minimal firing; low embodied energy |

## BMR Credit 4 — Alternate Wood-based Materials (1 pt New / N/A Existing)

**Requirements for 1 point:**
- ≥ 50% of all wood and wood-based products in the project are one of:
  - FSC-certified (Forest Stewardship Council) timber from sustainably managed forests
  - Reclaimed/salvaged timber
  - Non-wood alternative (bamboo, agri-waste boards, recycled composite panels)
  - Finger-jointed/engineered timber using small offcuts (MDF, particleboard with low formaldehyde)

**Prohibited:** Timber from illegally logged sources, old-growth forest timber.

---

## BMR Credit 5 — Handling of Waste Materials, During Construction (1 pt New / N/A Existing)

**Requirements for 1 point:**
- Construction site waste management plan developed before work begins
- C&D waste segregated on-site into: concrete/masonry, metals, timber, plastics, packaging, mixed/general
- ≥ 50% of C&D waste by weight diverted from landfill through recycling, reuse, or recovery
- Waste weighbridge tickets or recycler receipts maintained as documentation

**Note:** This credit is specifically about CONSTRUCTION PHASE waste management, not post-occupancy.
