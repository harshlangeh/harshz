**Rating System:** GRIHA V6
**Maximum Points:** 5 (Bonus — above 100 base points)
**Section Type:** Bonus / Innovation

---

## Criteria Summary

| Criterion | Points | Compliance |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| 11.1 — Innovation | 5 | Optional |

---

## Criterion 11.1 — Innovation (5 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To reward projects that demonstrate measurable environmental or social sustainability performance that goes significantly beyond current GRIHA V6 criteria, or achieves performance through a novel approach not yet captured in the rating system.

### Points Available

Up to **5 points** can be awarded for innovative strategies. Each innovation can earn 1–2 points, up to a maximum of 5 total. GRIHA evaluates each submission on its merits.

---

## What Qualifies as Innovation?

### Category A — Exemplary Performance (Performance beyond GRIHA thresholds)
Achieving a standard significantly higher than what earns maximum points in any GRIHA V6 section.

| Example | Innovation Basis |
|---------|-----------------|
| Net-Zero Energy Building (NZEB) | Energy section already rewarded; going to net-zero is beyond the scale |
| Net-Zero Water Building | Water self-sufficiency of 100%+ through on-site sources |
| Carbon Negative Construction | Embodied carbon sequestration exceeds embodied carbon emissions |
| 90%+ C&D Waste Diversion | Exceeds the 50% construction waste diversion in Section 2 |

### Category B — New Technology / Strategy Not in GRIHA V6
Deploying a technology or approach not currently assessed by GRIHA V6 criteria.

| Technology | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Building Integrated Photovoltaics (BIPV)** | Solar cells integrated into facade or glass, not just rooftop |
| **Phase Change Materials (PCM)** | Thermal storage in walls or ceilings to reduce HVAC loads |
| **Electrochromic Glass** | Smart glass that darkens automatically to reduce solar heat gain |
| **Vertical Farms / Productive Landscapes** | Food growing within or on the building |
| **Piezoelectric Floors** | Energy harvesting from pedestrian movement |
| **Green Walls (Planted facades)** | Significant insulation, biodiversity, and urban cooling benefits |
| **AI-based BMS** | Machine learning optimising HVAC based on occupancy patterns |
| **District Cooling/Heating** | Shared thermal plant serving multiple buildings |

### Category C — Social Innovation
Unique community or social benefit not captured in Socio-Economic Strategies section.

| Innovation | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Public green spaces open 24/7 | Parks, gardens, or waterbodies freely accessible to neighbourhood |
| Green building education centre | On-site visitor centre educating public on green features |
| Local artisan material procurement | Using traditional/local craft skills in building materials |

---

## How to Submit an Innovation Claim

1. **Prepare an Innovation Brief** (max 4 pages per innovation):
   - Problem statement / what existing GRIHA criteria doesn't capture
   - Description of the innovation with supporting data/calculations
   - Measurable environmental/social benefit (quantified)
   - Technical drawings, specifications, or case studies

2. **Submit during Documentation Stage:** Innovation briefs must be submitted with the design-stage documentation.

3. **GRIHA Evaluation:** The GRIHA Council technical team evaluates each brief and assigns 0–2 points per innovation at their discretion.

---

## Scoring Note

Innovation points are **bonus points** added to the 100 base points. They count toward the final star rating but are not required for any star threshold. A building scoring 70 base points + 5 innovation = 75 total → **4 Stars**.
