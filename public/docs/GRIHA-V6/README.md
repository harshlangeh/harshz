# GRIHA V6 — Green Rating for Integrated Habitat Assessment (Version 6)

> **Type:** Star Rating (1 to 5 Stars)
> **Total Points:** 100 (base) + 5 (innovation) = **105 pts**
> **Applies to:** New construction buildings in India

## Section Descriptions

### Section 1 — Sustainable Site Planning (12 pts)
Covers how the building site is planned and designed to minimise environmental impact. Includes green infrastructure design (trees, water bodies, permeable surfaces), low-impact design strategies (to reduce urban runoff and flooding), and measures to mitigate the Urban Heat Island Effect (UHIE) through cool roofs, reflective pavements, and shading.

**Key criteria:** Green Infrastructure (5 pts), Low Impact Design Strategies (5 pts), Design to Mitigate UHIE (2 pts)

### Section 2 — Construction Management (4 pts)
Addresses pollution and site management during the construction phase. Projects must control air and soil pollution from dust, machinery, and fuel. Topsoil must be stripped, stored, and reused. A documented construction management plan covering scheduling, waste, and equipment is expected.

**Key criteria:** Air and Soil Pollution Control (1 pt), Topsoil Preservation (1 pt), Construction Management Practices (2 pts)

### Section 3 — Energy Optimisation (17 pts)
The largest base section. Covers building energy performance through passive design (building orientation, shading, insulation) and active systems (efficient HVAC, lighting). Also rewards on-site renewable energy generation (solar PV, solar thermal, wind). Use of low-ODP and low-GWP refrigerants is mandatory.

**Key criteria:** Energy Optimization (12 pts), Renewable Energy Utilization (5 pts), Low ODP and GWP Materials (Mandatory)

### Section 4 — Occupant Comfort (13 pts)
Ensures the building provides a healthy and comfortable environment. Visual comfort evaluates daylight availability and glare control. Thermal and acoustic comfort looks at HVAC zoning, noise levels, and insulation. Indoor air quality (IAQ) criteria cover fresh air ventilation rates, CO₂ monitoring, and pollutant source control.

**Key criteria:** Visual Comfort (4 pts), Thermal & Acoustic Comfort (2 pts), Maintaining Indoor Air Quality (7 pts)

### Section 5 — Water Management (16 pts)
Comprehensive water strategy covering demand-side and supply-side. Demand reduction through low-flow fixtures and native landscaping. Wastewater treatment and reuse on-site. Rainwater management to reduce stormwater runoff and recharge groundwater. Water quality testing and targets for self-sufficiency.

**Key criteria:** Water Demand Reduction (4 pts), Wastewater Treatment (2 pts), Rainwater Management (5 pts), Water Quality and Self Sufficiency (5 pts)

### Section 6 — Solid Waste Management (6 pts)
Post-occupancy waste management: segregation at source (wet/dry/hazardous), recycling infrastructure, and organic waste treatment (composting or biogas) within or near the building site.

**Key criteria:** Waste Management – Post Occupancy (4 pts), Organic Waste Treatment (2 pts)

### Section 7 — Sustainable Building Materials (7 pts)
Credits for using alternative/recycled building materials such as fly ash bricks, AAC blocks, recycled aggregates, and industrial by-products in the building structure. Also covers use of alternative materials in external site works (paving, fencing).

**Key criteria:** Utilization of Alternative Materials in Building (5 pts), Alternative Materials for External Site Development (2 pts)

### Section 8 — Life Cycle Assessment and Costing (8 pts)
Rewards projects that conduct a life cycle assessment (LCA) to demonstrate reduced global warming potential of the building structure over its lifetime. Also covers life cycle cost analysis (LCCA) to evaluate long-term operational costs vs. upfront green investment.

**Key criteria:** Reduction in Global Warming Potential through LCA (5 pts), Life Cycle Cost Analysis (3 pts)

### Section 9 — Socio-Economic Strategies (7 pts)
Addresses the human and social dimensions of sustainable construction. Safety and sanitation provisions for construction workers during the build phase. Universal accessibility design for persons with disabilities. Dedicated service staff facilities. Positive social impact initiatives in the surrounding community.

**Key criteria:** Safety and Sanitation for Construction Workers (1 pt), Universal Accessibility (2 pts), Dedicated Facilities for Service Staff (2 pts), Positive Social Impact (2 pts)

### Section 10 — Performance Monitoring and Metering (10 pts)
Ensures the building performs as designed. Project commissioning covers testing and verification of all systems at handover. Smart metering tracks real-time energy and water consumption by zone. An operation and maintenance protocol (mandatory) ensures continued performance post-occupancy.

**Key criteria:** Project Commissioning (4 pts), Smart Metering and Monitoring (6 pts), Operation and Maintenance Protocol (Mandatory)

### Section 11 — Innovation (5 pts, Bonus)
Awards up to 5 additional points for technologies, practices, or strategies that go beyond the current GRIHA V6 requirements. Must demonstrate measurable environmental benefit not covered in sections 1–10.

**Key criteria:** Innovation (5 pts)

## Compliance Types

| Type | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Mandatory** | Must be achieved — project cannot be submitted without compliance |
| **Partly Mandatory** | Minimum performance threshold is mandatory; additional points available for exceeding it |
| **Optional** | Fully voluntary credit — choose to attempt or skip |
