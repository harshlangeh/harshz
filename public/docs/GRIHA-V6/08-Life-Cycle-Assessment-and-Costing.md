## Criterion 8.1 — Reduction in GWP through LCA (5 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To quantify and minimise the carbon footprint of the building's structure and envelope over its entire life cycle — from raw material extraction through manufacturing, construction, use, and demolition (cradle-to-grave).

### Points Scale

| GWP Reduction vs. Reference Building | Points |
|--------------------------------------|--------|
| ≥ 5% reduction | 1 |
| ≥ 10% reduction | 2 |
| ≥ 15% reduction | 3 |
| ≥ 20% reduction | 4 |
| ≥ 25% reduction | 5 |

### What is LCA?

**Life Cycle Assessment** (ISO 14040/14044) evaluates environmental impacts across all stages:

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **A1-A3: Product** | Raw material extraction, transport to factory, manufacturing |
| **A4-A5: Construction** | Transport to site, on-site construction process |
| **B1-B7: Use** | In-use emissions (operational energy, maintenance, replacement) |
| **C1-C4: End of Life** | Demolition, transport, waste processing |
| **D: Beyond boundary** | Recycling benefits / reuse potential |

### Global Warming Potential (GWP)
- Expressed in **kg CO₂-equivalent per m² of floor area**
- Typical Indian office building: 250–450 kg CO₂-eq/m² (embodied carbon, cradle-to-practical completion)
- Use of fly ash, recycled steel, and reduced concrete can cut this by 15–30%

| Tool | Type | Cost |
|------|------|------|
| **One Click LCA** | Cloud-based | Paid |
| **SimaPro** | Desktop | Paid |
| **OpenLCA** | Desktop | Free/Open source |
| **GaBi** | Desktop | Paid |

### Documentation Required
- LCA report prepared by a qualified professional
- Bill of Materials (BOM) with material quantities
- EPD (Environmental Product Declarations) for key materials (if available)
- Comparison of GWP: reference building vs. proposed building (% reduction)

## Criterion 8.2 — Life Cycle Cost Analysis (3 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To help building owners understand the total cost of ownership over the building's life span, enabling better decisions about upfront investments in sustainable features.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| LCCA conducted covering at least: capital cost, energy costs, water costs, maintenance costs, and replacement costs over 25 years | 2 |
| LCCA demonstrates positive NPV for green measures vs. conventional baseline over 25-year period | 1 |
| **Total** | **3** |

### Life Cycle Cost Components

| Cost Category | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Capital Cost (CAPEX)** | Construction, equipment, installation |
| **Operation Cost (OPEX)** | Energy bills, water bills, cleaning |
| **Maintenance Cost** | Preventive maintenance, servicing contracts |
| **Replacement Cost** | Equipment replacement over life span |
| **End-of-Life Cost** | Demolition and disposal |

### Net Present Value (NPV) Calculation
```
NPV = Σ [Cash Flow(t) / (1 + discount rate)^t]

Where:
- t = year (1 to 25)
- Discount rate = typically 8–12% for India
- Cash Flow = Annual savings from green measures (energy, water, maintenance)
```

### Typical Green Measure Paybacks (India)

| Measure | Typical Payback |
|---------|----------------|
| LED lighting retrofit | 2–4 years |
| Solar PV rooftop | 5–7 years |
| Solar water heating | 3–5 years |
| High-efficiency HVAC | 5–8 years |
| Rainwater harvesting | 4–7 years |
| Green roof (insulation) | 10–15 years |

### Documentation Required
- LCCA spreadsheet/report with assumptions clearly stated
- Discount rate and escalation rates used (energy, water tariffs)
- Comparison table: conventional design vs. green design (25-year NPV)
