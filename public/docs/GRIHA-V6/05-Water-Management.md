## Criterion 5.1 — Water Demand Reduction (4 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To reduce potable water consumption through efficient fixtures, fittings, and landscaping practices.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| All indoor plumbing fixtures meet efficiency targets (see table below) | 2 |
| Landscaping uses only native/adaptive species requiring no permanent irrigation | 1 |
| On-site water recycling for flushing/irrigation (non-potable reuse) | 1 |
| **Total** | **4** |

### Fixture Flow Rate Targets

| Fixture | Max Flow Rate |
|---------|--------------|
| WC cistern (dual flush) | 3 L / 6 L (half/full flush) |
| Urinal (flush valve) | ≤ 1.5 L/flush |
| Washbasin tap | ≤ 6 L/min |
| Kitchen sink tap | ≤ 8 L/min |
| Shower head | ≤ 8 L/min |

### Baseline Consumption Comparison

| Fixture Type | Conventional | Efficient | Savings |
|-------------|-------------|----------|---------|
| WC (per flush) | 9 L | 4.5 L avg | ~50% |
| Washbasin tap | 12 L/min | 6 L/min | ~50% |
| Showerhead | 15 L/min | 8 L/min | ~47% |

## Criterion 5.2 — Wastewater Treatment (2 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To treat all or part of the building's wastewater on-site and reuse it for non-potable applications, reducing discharge to municipal sewers.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| On-site STP (Sewage Treatment Plant) treating ≥ 50% of wastewater; treated water reused for flushing/irrigation | 2 |
| **Total** | **2** |

### Acceptable STP Technologies

| Technology | Scale | Footprint |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Sequential Batch Reactor (SBR) | Medium–Large | Moderate |
| Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor (MBBR) | Small–Large | Compact |
| Extended Aeration (EA) | Large | Large |
| Membrane Bioreactor (MBR) | Small–Large | Very compact, high quality output |
| Biogas (anaerobic + aerobic) | Medium | Moderate |

### Treated Water Reuse Applications
- Toilet flushing (WC and urinals) — most common reuse
- Drip irrigation of landscape
- Cooling tower makeup water (tertiary treatment required)
- Car washing

### Documentation Required
- STP design and capacity calculations
- Water balance showing percentage of wastewater treated and reused
- Treated water quality test reports (post-treatment)

## Criterion 5.3 — Rainwater Management (5 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To capture, store, and utilise rainwater on-site, reducing dependence on municipal supply and recharging groundwater.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Rainwater harvesting system designed to capture 100% of roof runoff (based on 24-hr design storm) | 2 |
| Storage tank sized for ≥ 10 days of non-potable demand | 1 |
| Groundwater recharge pits/percolation wells provided | 1 |
| Rainwater used for at least one potable or non-potable end use (after filtration) | 1 |
| **Total** | **5** |

### Rainwater Harvesting Design

**Step 1: Calculate Roof Runoff**
```
Runoff (L) = Rainfall (mm) × Roof Area (m²) × Runoff Coefficient × 0.85 (filter loss)
```
- Runoff Coefficient for RCC roof: 0.85–0.90
- Example: 50 mm rainfall × 1000 m² × 0.85 × 0.85 = 36,125 L per rain event

**Step 2: Size Storage Tank**
- Minimum: 10-day non-potable demand
- Non-potable demand ≈ 45 L/person/day (flushing + garden) for offices

**Step 3: Filtration**
- First-flush diverter: Discard first 1–2 mm of roof rainfall (carries pollutants)
- Sand filter or rapid gravity filter before storage
- UV treatment if used for drinking

### Documentation Required
- Rainwater harvesting design drawings
- Storage tank sizing calculations
- Water balance showing annual harvesting potential vs. demand

## Criterion 5.4 — Water Quality and Self Sufficiency (5 pts, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To ensure the building has access to safe water and progressively reduces its dependence on municipal or external water supply.

### Mandatory Requirement
All drinking water provided on-site must meet IS 10500:2012 (Indian Standard for Drinking Water Quality). A water quality testing report by a NABL-accredited lab is mandatory.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Drinking water quality tested and meets IS 10500 standards | Mandatory |
| Water metering installed for all major end uses (domestic, HVAC, irrigation, STP) | 1 |
| Water audit trail maintained (monthly consumption logs by end use) | 1 |
| Water self-sufficiency ≥ 30% (on-site sources cover ≥ 30% of total demand) | 1 |
| Water self-sufficiency ≥ 50% | 2 |
| Water self-sufficiency ≥ 75% | 3 |
| **Total (excl. mandatory)** | **5** |

### Water Balance Example

| Source | Volume/Month |
|--------|-------------|
| Municipal supply | 100 m³ |
| Rainwater harvested | 30 m³ |
| STP treated water reused | 25 m³ |
| **Total on-site** | **55 m³ / 155 m³ = 35% self-sufficient** |

### Documentation Required
- NABL water quality test report
- Sub-meter locations on plumbing drawings
- Water balance calculation (supply sources vs. demand by end use)
- Monthly water consumption logs (for existing buildings)
