## Criterion 6.1 — Waste Management – Post Occupancy (4 pts, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To ensure that building occupants have access to proper waste segregation infrastructure and that waste is diverted from landfill through recycling, composting, and other diversion methods.

### Mandatory Requirement
Segregation of waste into at least **wet (organic), dry (recyclable), and hazardous (e-waste, batteries)** categories must be provided. This is in line with the **Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016** (India).

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Colour-coded bins provided at all floors/zones (green = organic, blue = dry, red = hazardous) | 1 |
| Dedicated waste storage room with adequate ventilation at building level | 1 |
| Formal waste collection agreement with a registered recycler/aggregator | 1 |
| Waste data tracked and reported monthly (weight by category) | 1 |
| **Total** | **4** |

### Waste Categories and Bin Colours (as per SWM Rules 2016)

| Category | Bin Colour | Examples |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Wet/Organic | **Green** | Food scraps, garden trimmings, tea/coffee waste |
| Dry/Recyclable | **Blue** | Paper, cardboard, plastic bottles, glass, metal cans |
| Hazardous/Domestic | **Red** | Batteries, light bulbs, paint, pharmaceuticals |
| E-Waste | **Black/Grey** | Old electronics, cables, phones |

### Waste Reduction Hierarchy
1. **Reduce** — Buy less, purchase in bulk, go paperless
2. **Reuse** — Repair, repurpose, donate instead of discarding
3. **Recycle** — Properly segregate for collection
4. **Recover** — Energy from waste (last resort before landfill)
5. **Dispose** — Landfill (must be minimised)

### Documentation Required
- Floor plan showing bin locations at all floors
- Waste storage room drawings with dimensions and ventilation details
- Signed agreement with registered waste collection/recycling agency
- Monthly waste generation log (kg/category)

## Criterion 6.2 — Organic Waste Treatment (2 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To treat the organic fraction of waste on-site or nearby, converting it into useful outputs (compost or biogas) rather than sending it to landfill where it generates methane.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| On-site organic waste treatment with capacity to handle ≥ 50% of organic waste generated | 1 |
| On-site organic waste treatment with capacity to handle 100% of organic waste generated | 2 |
| **Total** | **2** |

### Eligible Technologies

| Technology | Output | Best For |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Composting (aerobic)** | Compost/manure | Buildings with garden areas |
| **Vermicomposting** | High-quality compost | Medium-scale offices, institutions |
| **Biogas digester (anaerobic)** | Biogas + slurry | Canteens, hotels, large buildings |
| **In-vessel composter** | Compost | Space-constrained urban buildings |
| **OWC (Organic Waste Converter)** | Compost | Fast processing, compact |

### Organic Waste Generation Estimates

| Building Type | Organic Waste |
|--------------|--------------|
| Office (with canteen) | 0.1–0.3 kg/person/day |
| Hotel | 0.5–1.0 kg/room/day |
| Residential | 0.2–0.4 kg/unit/day |
| School/College | 0.05–0.15 kg/student/day |

### System Sizing Example
- 200-person office with canteen
- Organic waste ≈ 200 × 0.2 kg = 40 kg/day
- Biogas digester size needed: 40 kg × 3 (HRT factor) = 120 kg capacity
- Or: In-vessel composter with ≥ 40 kg/day throughput

### Documentation Required
- Equipment specifications and capacity
- Waste balance showing organic fraction and treatment capacity
- Site plan showing location of treatment equipment
- End-use plan for compost/biogas produced
