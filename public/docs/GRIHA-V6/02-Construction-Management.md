## Criterion 2.1 — Air and Soil Pollution Control (1 pt, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To minimise air and soil contamination during construction through best-practice site management.

### Mandatory Requirement
The construction site must comply with applicable environmental regulations for dust control and sediment runoff. A basic erosion and sediment control (ESC) plan must be submitted.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Comprehensive dust suppression plan implemented (water spraying, wind barriers, covered vehicles) AND soil erosion controls in place (silt fences, check dams) | 1 |
| **Total** | **1** |

### Documentation Required
- Construction site photographs showing dust control measures
- Erosion and sediment control plan
- Site manager's declaration/log of dust suppression activities

---

## Criterion 2.2 — Topsoil Preservation (1 pt, Optional)

### Intent
To preserve valuable topsoil — which takes centuries to form — for reuse in post-construction landscaping rather than disposing of it as waste.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Topsoil stripped and stockpiled at the start of construction, then reused on-site for landscaping | 1 |
| **Total** | **1** |

### Requirements
- Topsoil (typically the top 150–300 mm) must be stripped before excavation begins
- Stockpile must be maintained away from drainage paths
- Stockpile must not exceed 2 m height (to prevent compaction and anaerobic degradation)
- Reused on-site — not sold or disposed off-site

### Documentation Required
- Site photographs showing topsoil stripping and stockpiling
- Landscape plan showing areas where topsoil is reinstated
- Calculation of topsoil volume stripped and reused

## Criterion 2.3 — Construction Management Practices (2 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To ensure construction is conducted in an organised, low-impact manner that minimises waste, noise, and nuisance to the surrounding environment and community.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Documented Construction Management Plan (CMP) covering waste, scheduling, noise, and site logistics | 1 |
| Post-construction C&D waste diversion rate ≥ 50% (by weight) from landfill through recycling or reuse | 1 |
| **Total** | **2** |

### What a Construction Management Plan Should Cover
1. **Waste Management:** Segregation of C&D waste (concrete, steel, timber, packaging), recycling targets, designated waste zones
2. **Dust and Noise Control:** Hours of noisy operations, community notification procedures
3. **Traffic and Logistics:** Construction vehicle routing, material delivery scheduling
4. **Worker Health and Safety:** PPE requirements, first aid facilities, site inductions
5. **Environmental Monitoring:** Regular site inspections and incident reporting

### C&D Waste Types and Diversion Options

| Waste Type | Diversion Option |
|------------|-----------------|
| Concrete rubble | Crush and reuse as sub-base aggregate |
| Steel offcuts | Return to scrap dealer/recycler |
| Timber formwork | Reuse in other projects |
| Packaging (plastic, cardboard) | Separate and recycle |
| Excavated soil (non-topsoil) | Use as fill material on-site |

### Documentation Required
- Copy of the Construction Management Plan
- Waste tracking log (materials in and diverted)
- Weight certificates or receipts from recyclers
- Site photographs at various stages
