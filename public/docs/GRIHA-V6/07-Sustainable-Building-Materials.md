## Criterion 7.1 — Utilization of Alternative Materials in Building (5 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To promote the use of building materials that incorporate industrial by-products or recycled content, reducing pressure on virgin natural resources and cutting the embodied energy and carbon of the building structure.

### Points Scale (% of total structural and masonry materials by cost or volume)

| % Alternative Materials Used | Points |
|-----------------------------|--------|
| ≥ 10% | 1 |
| ≥ 20% | 2 |
| ≥ 30% | 3 |
| ≥ 40% | 4 |
| ≥ 50% | 5 |

### Eligible Alternative Materials

| Material | Replaces | Waste Source |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Fly Ash Bricks** | Clay bricks | Coal power plant residue |
| **AAC Blocks (Autoclaved Aerated Concrete)** | Conventional blocks | Fly ash + cement + aluminium |
| **Fly Ash in Concrete (PPC cement)** | OPC concrete | Coal combustion residue |
| **GGBS (Ground Granulated Blast Slag)** | OPC | Iron & steel industry slag |
| **Recycled Aggregate** | Virgin stone aggregate | C&D waste concrete |
| **Bamboo** (engineered) | Steel/timber | Rapidly renewable |
| **Ferro-cement** | RCC slabs | High-fly-ash mortar |
| **Recycled Steel** | Primary steel | Scrap metal recycling |

### Why Fly Ash is Popular in India
- India generates ~200 million tonnes of fly ash per year from coal power plants
- Fly ash bricks consume 60–70% less energy to produce than clay bricks
- Clay brick kilns destroy agricultural topsoil — fly ash bricks avoid this
- PPC cement (with fly ash) is now the standard cement in India

### Embodied Energy Comparison

| Material | Embodied Energy (MJ/kg) |
|---------|------------------------|
| OPC Concrete | 1.2 – 1.5 |
| PPC Concrete (30% fly ash) | 0.9 – 1.1 |
| Clay bricks | 2.0 – 3.5 |
| Fly ash bricks | 0.8 – 1.2 |
| Steel (recycled) | 8 – 10 |
| Steel (primary/virgin) | 25 – 35 |

### Documentation Required
- Bill of Quantities (BOQ) with materials listed
- Cost breakdown of alternative vs. conventional materials (percentage calculation)
- Manufacturer's product data sheets confirming alternative content
- IS certification or BIS mark for fly ash/AAC products

## Criterion 7.2 — Alternative Materials for External Site Development (2 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To extend sustainable material use to external works: paving, compound walls, pathways, parking areas, and site furniture.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| ≥ 25% of external paving area uses permeable or alternative materials | 1 |
| ≥ 50% of external paving/site elements use alternative materials | 2 |
| **Total** | **2** |

### Eligible External Materials

| Application | Alternative Material |
|------------|---------------------|
| Pathways/Footpaths | Fly ash paver blocks, recycled concrete pavers |
| Parking | Grass pavers, gravel paving, permeable interlocking concrete pavers (PICP) |
| Compound wall | Fly ash blocks, rammed earth, recycled brick |
| Kerbing | Recycled concrete kerbs |
| Site furniture | Recycled plastic lumber, bamboo |
| Drain covers | Recycled HDPE, recycled metal |

### Dual Benefit: Permeable + Alternative
Many alternative external materials are also permeable (grass pavers, gravel paving, PICP), earning points in both this section **and** Section 5.3 (Rainwater Management) simultaneously.

### Documentation Required
- Site development plan with material specifications
- Area calculations for alternative vs. conventional external materials
- Product data sheets with recycled content information
