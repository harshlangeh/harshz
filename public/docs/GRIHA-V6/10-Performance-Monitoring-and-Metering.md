## Criterion 10.1 — Project Commissioning (4 pts, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To verify that all building systems are installed, calibrated, and operating as designed before the building is handed over to occupants — preventing the performance gap between design intent and actual performance.

### Mandatory Requirement
All HVAC and electrical systems must be commissioned and tested by the contractor before final handover. Commissioning reports must be submitted.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Independent Commissioning Authority (CxA) appointed and commissioning plan submitted pre-construction | 1 |
| Systems commissioning complete: HVAC, electrical, plumbing, fire protection | 2 |
| Post-occupancy commissioning (re-commissioning at 12 months to check actual vs. design performance) | 1 |
| **Total** | **4** |

### What is Commissioning?

Commissioning is the systematic process of ensuring that all systems in a building are designed, installed, functionally tested, and capable of being operated and maintained to perform in conformity with the design intent.

### Systems That Must Be Commissioned

| System | What is Tested |
|--------|---------------|
| **HVAC** | Airflow rates (CFM), temperatures (supply/return/zone), controls sequencing, balancing |
| **Electrical** | Power factor, circuit loading, lighting control systems, emergency power |
| **Plumbing** | Hot/cold water pressure and flow, water heater performance, pump curves |
| **Fire Protection** | Sprinkler activation test, fire alarm panel, smoke detectors, fire pump |
| **BMS/Controls** | All automated sequences, sensor calibration, setpoint verification |
| **Renewable Energy** | Solar PV output vs. design generation, inverter performance |

### Documentation Required
- Commissioning plan (pre-construction)
- Commissioning authority appointment letter
- System-by-system commissioning reports with test results
- Deficiency log and resolution records
- Post-occupancy commissioning report (1 year post-handover)

## Criterion 10.2 — Smart Metering and Monitoring (6 pts, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To install a granular metering infrastructure that enables building operators to track energy and water consumption in real time, identify waste, and demonstrate actual green performance.

### Mandatory Requirement
Main utility meters (electricity and water) must be connected to a Building Management System (BMS) or data logger. Monthly consumption reports must be generated.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Electricity sub-metering by zone/floor or major end-use (HVAC, lighting, plug loads) | 2 |
| Water sub-metering by end-use (domestic, HVAC, irrigation, STP) | 1 |
| Energy dashboard accessible to facility management (real-time and historical data) | 1 |
| BMS integrates all building systems with automated alerts for abnormal consumption | 1 |
| Energy data benchmarked against GRIHA/ECBC targets; monthly reports generated | 1 |
| **Total** | **6** |

### Metering Infrastructure

**Electricity Metering Hierarchy:**
```
Main Utility Meter (HT/LT)
    ├── HVAC Chiller Plant
    ├── HVAC AHU / FCUs (Floor-wise)
    ├── Lighting (Zone-wise or Floor-wise)
    ├── Plug Loads / Power Sockets
    ├── Common Areas (Lift, Corridor, Basement)
    └── Renewable Energy Generation (Solar PV)
```

**Water Metering Hierarchy:**
```
Municipal Supply Main Meter
    ├── Domestic Potable Use (drinking, washrooms)
    ├── HVAC Cooling Tower Makeup
    ├── Irrigation
    └── STP Inflow / Reuse Water
```

### Energy Performance Indicators (EPIs) to Track

| Indicator | Unit | Good Benchmark (Office) |
|-----------|------|------------------------|
| Energy Use Intensity (EUI) | kWh/m²/year | ≤ 120 kWh/m²/year |
| HVAC EPI | kWh/m²/year | ≤ 70 kWh/m²/year |
| Lighting EPI | kWh/m²/year | ≤ 15 kWh/m²/year |
| Water Consumption | L/person/day | ≤ 45 L/person/day (non-potable) |

### BMS Features Expected
- Real-time display of all sub-meter readings
- Automated alerts when consumption exceeds threshold (e.g., >120% of previous month)
- Scheduled reports (daily, monthly, annual)
- Historical trend graphs
- Integration with renewable energy generation data

### Documentation Required
- Metering and monitoring plan drawing (showing all sub-meter locations)
- BMS architecture diagram
- Sample energy dashboard screenshots
- 12-month consumption data (for post-occupancy assessment)

## Criterion 10.3 — Operation and Maintenance Protocol (Mandatory, 0 pts)

### Intent
To ensure that all sustainable systems in the building are properly maintained after handover so that green performance is sustained over the building's lifetime.

### Mandatory Requirements (No Points — Must Comply)

An **Operations and Maintenance (O&M) Manual** must be prepared and handed over to the building owner/facility manager. It must include:

1. **System Descriptions** — How each major system works, its design parameters
2. **Preventive Maintenance Schedule** — What needs to be serviced and how often
3. **Troubleshooting Guide** — Common problems and solutions for each system
4. **Emergency Procedures** — Contacts and actions for system failures
5. **Energy and Water Targets** — Monthly/annual consumption targets for all systems
6. **Green System Guidance** — How to operate solar panels, STP, rainwater harvesting, BMS

### Maintenance Frequency Guide

| System | Frequency |
|--------|-----------|
| HVAC filters | Monthly (or as per manufacturer) |
| Chiller maintenance | Semi-annual |
| Fire systems test | Quarterly |
| Solar PV panel cleaning | Monthly (or after heavy rain) |
| STP servicing | Weekly inspection; monthly report |
| Rainwater filter cleaning | Before monsoon and after each major storm |
| BMS calibration | Annual |
| Water quality testing | Quarterly (IS 10500 parameters) |

### Documentation Required
- Complete O&M Manual (bound copy + digital)
- Acknowledgement of receipt by building owner/facility manager
- Training session conducted for facility management team (attendance records)
