## Criterion 9.1 — Safety and Sanitation for Construction Workers (1 pt, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To protect the health, safety, and dignity of construction workers — one of India's most vulnerable labour groups — through on-site provisions and compliance with labour law.

### Mandatory Requirement
Compliance with the **Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996** and applicable state rules. All workers must be registered.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Documented safety and sanitation plan implemented covering: PPE provision, first aid, separate male/female toilets, clean drinking water, and site induction training | 1 |
| **Total** | **1** |

### Minimum Facilities Required

| Facility | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Drinking water | Safe potable water accessible to all workers |
| Toilets | 1 per 25 workers; separate facilities for women |
| Washing facility | Wash basins near toilet areas |
| First aid | First aid box on-site; trained first-aider present |
| PPE | Helmets, safety boots, gloves, harness for height work |
| Site induction | All workers briefed on safety rules before starting work |

### Documentation Required
- Site safety plan
- Photographs of facilities (toilets, drinking water, PPE)
- Worker registration records under BOCW Act
- Training attendance register for site induction

## Criterion 9.2 — Universal Accessibility (2 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To ensure the building is usable by persons with disabilities (PwD), elderly occupants, and pregnant women — in line with the **Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016** (RPWD Act) and **Harmonised Guidelines (2021)** by MoHUA.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Accessible entrance, ramps, signage, and at least one accessible toilet on each floor | 1 |
| Full compliance with Harmonised Guidelines 2021 across all public areas + accessible parking | 2 |
| **Total** | **2** |

### Key Universal Design Requirements

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| **Entrance ramp** | Slope ≤ 1:12 (8.3%), min width 1.5 m, handrails both sides |
| **Doorways** | Clear width ≥ 900 mm |
| **Corridors** | Minimum 1.5 m clear width |
| **Lifts** | Min 1100 mm × 1400 mm car size; braille panel, audio announcement |
| **Toilets (accessible)** | 1800 mm × 1750 mm minimum; grab bars; outward-opening door |
| **Parking** | ≥ 2 accessible bays (4.8 m wide), marked with ISA symbol, closest to entrance |
| **Tactile paving** | Warning tiles at hazard locations; directional tiles at key routes |

### Seven Principles of Universal Design
1. Equitable Use
2. Flexibility in Use
3. Simple and Intuitive Use
4. Perceptible Information
5. Tolerance for Error
6. Low Physical Effort
7. Size and Space for Approach and Use

### Documentation Required
- Architectural drawings showing all accessibility features
- Compliance checklist against Harmonised Guidelines 2021

## Criterion 9.3 — Dedicated Facilities for Service Staff (2 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To provide dignified, functional facilities for housekeeping, security, maintenance, and other service staff who support the building's daily operations.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Dedicated staff rest room, changing room/lockers, and toilet facilities (separate from public areas) | 1 |
| Staff canteen/pantry/eating area with hygienic food preparation facilities | 1 |
| **Total** | **2** |

### Minimum Staff Facilities

| Facility | Area / Standard |
|---------|----------------|
| Rest room | Min 1.5 m²/person; seating, ventilation |
| Changing room | Lockers or hooks; separate male/female |
| Toilets | Dedicated staff toilets at back-of-house |
| Eating area | Seating + microwave or hotplate; clean, ventilated |
| First aid | Accessible to all staff |

### Documentation Required
- Floor plan showing staff facility locations
- Area calculations and occupancy count for service staff
- Photographs (for existing buildings)

## Criterion 9.4 — Positive Social Impact (2 pts, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To ensure the building project contributes positively to the surrounding neighbourhood and community, beyond just minimising its negative impacts.

### Mandatory Requirement
The project must not have displaced local communities or encroached on public green space or water bodies without proper rehabilitation measures.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Community engagement programme conducted during design phase; community concerns incorporated | 1 |
| Active CSR programme linked to the building (local employment, skill development, green education) | 1 |
| **Total** | **2** |

### Examples of Positive Social Impact Initiatives
- **Local Employment:** Priority hiring of skilled/semi-skilled workers from within 50 km radius
- **Skill Development:** Training sessions for local construction workers in green building techniques
- **Green Education:** Awareness programmes for local schools, colleges, or community groups
- **Public Green Space:** Opening rooftop gardens, parks, or water features to the public
- **Supply Chain:** Procuring local materials and products to support regional economy

### Documentation Required
- Community engagement report with attendance records
- CSR programme description and outcomes (number of beneficiaries)
- Any signed MOUs or agreements with local community organisations
