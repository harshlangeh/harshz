## Criterion 1.1 — Green Infrastructure (5 pts, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To preserve and enhance the natural ecology of the site through strategic landscape design, biodiversity protection, and permeable surface coverage.

### Mandatory Requirement
A minimum green cover (trees, shrubs, groundcover) must be provided as per local bye-laws. Projects must submit a site landscape plan stamped by a registered landscape architect.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Site green cover ≥ 20% of total site area | 1 |
| Site green cover ≥ 30% of total site area | 2 |
| Native/adaptive species ≥ 50% of all planted species | 1 |
| Permeable surfaces ≥ 30% of non-built area | 1 |
| **Total** | **5** |

### Documentation Required
- Landscape plan with species list and canopy coverage calculation
- Percentage of native species (scientific names required)
- Permeable paving specification or soil permeability report

## Criterion 1.2 — Low Impact Design Strategies (5 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To reduce stormwater runoff, flooding, and groundwater depletion through design measures that mimic natural hydrological cycles.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| On-site stormwater retention/detention to manage 24-hr rainfall event | 2 |
| Bioswales, rain gardens, or vegetated filter strips provided | 1 |
| Permeable paving for parking and pedestrian areas | 1 |
| No net increase in stormwater runoff from pre-development condition | 1 |
| **Total** | **5** |

### Key Definitions
- **Stormwater retention:** Water stored on-site and not released (e.g., harvesting tank)
- **Stormwater detention:** Water temporarily stored and released slowly after the storm event
- **Bioswale:** Vegetated channel designed to slow and filter stormwater runoff

### Documentation Required
- Hydrological analysis comparing pre- and post-development runoff
- Stormwater management plan (SMP) with design calculations
- Technical drawings of all LID features (bioswales, permeable paving, etc.)

## Criterion 1.3 — Design to Mitigate UHIE (2 pts, Optional)

### Intent
To reduce the Urban Heat Island Effect (UHIE) caused by dark impervious surfaces and reduced vegetation, which raise ambient air temperatures in urban areas.

### Points Available

| Sub-criterion | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| Roof SRI ≥ 78 for ≥ 50% of total roof area | 1 |
| Non-roof hard paved surfaces have SRI ≥ 29, OR ≥ 50% of parking area shaded | 1 |
| **Total** | **2** |

### Key Definitions
- **SRI (Solar Reflectance Index):** A measure of a surface's ability to reflect solar heat. Higher SRI = cooler surface.
  - White/light-coloured surfaces: SRI 78–100
  - Dark surfaces: SRI 0–25
- **Cool Roof:** A roof system with high SRI that reduces heat gain into the building
- **UHIE:** Urban areas are typically 2–5°C warmer than surrounding rural areas due to heat-absorbing surfaces

### Common Cool Roof Products
- White cement/lime wash (SRI ~90)
- High-reflectance roof coatings
- China mosaic tiles
- White UPVC membranes

### Documentation Required
- Roof plan with material specifications and SRI values
- Manufacturer's test reports for SRI values of roofing and paving materials
- Site plan showing shaded parking areas (with shading device details)
