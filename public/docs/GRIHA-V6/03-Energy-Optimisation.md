## Criterion 3.1 — Energy Optimization (12 pts, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To significantly reduce building energy consumption through a combination of passive design strategies and efficient active systems compared to a reference building baseline.

### Mandatory Requirement
The building must meet the minimum energy performance prescribed in the **National Building Code (NBC) Part 11** and/or **ECBC (Energy Conservation Building Code)**. The project cannot be rated without meeting this minimum.

### Points Scale (% improvement over ECBC/NBC baseline)

| Energy Savings vs. Baseline | Points |
|-----------------------------|--------|
| 10% improvement | 1 |
| 15% improvement | 2 |
| 20% improvement | 4 |
| 25% improvement | 6 |
| 30% improvement | 8 |
| 35% improvement | 10 |
| 40% or more improvement | 12 |

### Passive Design Strategies (Climate-Responsive)

**Composite Climate (Delhi, Agra, Lucknow):**
- East-West building orientation to reduce solar gain on long facades
- External shading devices (chajjas, fins, overhangs) on East and West facades
- High thermal mass walls (240 mm brick or equivalent)
- Roof insulation (EPS/XPS, R-value ≥ 3.5 m²K/W)

**Hot and Dry Climate (Jodhpur, Ahmedabad):**
- Small window-to-wall ratio (WWR ≤ 20%)
- Cavity walls or insulated walls
- Courtyards and wind towers for natural ventilation
- Evaporative cooling potential

**Warm and Humid Climate (Mumbai, Chennai, Kochi):**
- Cross-ventilation design (openings on opposite/adjacent facades)
- Larger windows with external shading
- Lower thermal mass preferred
- High-performance glazing (Low-e coatings)

### Active System Efficiency Requirements

| System | Minimum Efficiency |
|--------|-------------------|
| Air Conditioning | As per BEE star rating (5-star preferred) |
| Chillers | COP ≥ 5.0 (for large buildings) |
| Lighting (interior) | ≤ 5 W/m² for office (LPD targets per NBC) |
| Lighting (exterior) | LED with lighting controls |
| Pumps and fans | IE3 motors minimum |
| Lifts/Elevators | Regenerative drives preferred |

### Energy Simulation Tools Accepted
- EnergyPlus
- eQUEST
- IES-VE
- DesignBuilder
- DOE-2

### Documentation Required
- Energy simulation report with baseline and proposed model comparison
- Equipment schedules with efficiency ratings
- Building envelope specifications (U-values, SHGC, VLT of glazing)
- BEE star rating certificates for all major equipment

## Criterion 3.2 — Renewable Energy Utilization (5 pts, Partly Mandatory)

### Intent
To reduce dependence on grid electricity by generating clean energy on-site from renewable sources.

### Mandatory Requirement
All refrigerants and insulation materials used in the building must be zero ODP (Ozone Depletion Potential). This is captured under Criterion 3.3 but is a prerequisite for this section as well.

### Points Scale (% of building annual energy from renewables)

| Renewable Energy % | Points |
|--------------------|--------|
| 1% of total energy demand met on-site | 1 |
| 5% of total energy demand met on-site | 2 |
| 10% of total energy demand met on-site | 3 |
| 15% of total energy demand met on-site | 4 |
| 20% or more | 5 |

### Eligible Renewable Technologies

| Technology | Application |
|------------|------------|
| **Solar PV** | Rooftop, facade-integrated, or carport-mounted |
| **Solar Thermal** | Hot water for domestic use, process heat |
| **Wind Turbines** | Small-scale on-site turbines (if feasible) |
| **Biomass/Biogas** | On-site organic waste converted to energy |
| **Ground Source Heat Pump** | Geothermal for HVAC (in suitable locations) |

### Solar PV Sizing Guidance
- Typical office building: 10–15 kWp per 1,000 m² roof area
- 1 kWp ≈ 1,400–1,600 units/year in most Indian climates
- Payback period: 5–7 years with subsidies

### Documentation Required
- Renewable energy generation calculation (annual kWh)
- System design drawings and equipment specifications
- Calculation of % renewable energy vs. annual building energy demand
- Net metering agreement (if grid-connected solar)

## Criterion 3.3 — Low ODP and GWP Materials (Mandatory, 0 pts)

### Intent
To eliminate substances that damage the stratospheric ozone layer and contribute to global warming through refrigerant and insulation material selection.

### Mandatory Requirements (Must Comply — No Points, No Exceptions)

**Refrigerants:**
- All HVAC refrigerants must have ODP = 0
- GWP of refrigerants should be as low as practicable
- R-22 (HCFC) is **prohibited**
- Acceptable refrigerants: R-32, R-410A, R-134a, R-717 (Ammonia), R-290 (Propane), R-744 (CO₂)

**Insulation Materials:**
- CFC-blown and HCFC-blown foam insulation is **prohibited**
- Acceptable insulations: EPS, XPS (CO₂-blown), mineral wool, glass wool, aerogel

### Ozone Layer Basics
- **ODP (Ozone Depletion Potential):** Relative measure vs. R-11 (reference = 1.0). R-22 ODP = 0.055. All modern refrigerants target ODP = 0.
- **GWP (Global Warming Potential):** CO₂ equivalent over 100 years. R-410A GWP = 2088. R-32 GWP = 675. Natural refrigerants (R-717, R-290) have GWP < 5.

### Documentation Required
- HVAC equipment specifications confirming refrigerant type and ODP/GWP values
- Insulation material specifications with ODP confirmation
- MEP engineer's declaration of compliance
